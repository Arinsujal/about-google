History of Google

Google LLC is an american multinational tech. Giant that focuses on AI, Search Engine, Online adv., Cloud Computing, Quantum Computing and many more.

  • Google was founded on September 4, 1998, by Larry Page and Sergey Brin.
  • Google was reorganized as a wholly owned subsidiary of Alphabet Inc.
  • Sundar Pichai was appointed CEO of Google on October 24, 2015, replacing Larry Page, who became the CEO of Alphabet.
  • On December 3, 2019, Pichai also became the CEO of Alphabet.
  • Google began selling advertisements associated with search keywords against Page and Brin's initial opposition toward an advertising-funded search engine.
  • In 2004,The sale of $1.67 billion shares gave Google a market capitalization of more than $23 billion.
  • By 2011, Google was handling approximately 3 billion searches per day.
  • According to Interbrand's annual Best Global Brands report, Google has been the second most valuable brand in the world (behind Apple Inc.) in 2013,2014,2015, and 2016, with a valuation of $133 billion.

Revenue System

How does Google make money?

  • The main way Google generates its revenue is through a pair of advertising services called Ads and AdSense.
  • AdSense is similar, except that instead of displaying ads on a Google SERP, a webmaster can choose to integrate ads onto a site.
  • Every time someone clicks on an ad on the site, the site receives a portion of the ad revenue (and Google gets the rest).
  • Google Analytics is a powerful tool that tracks traffic to websites, enabling them to better understand who their users are and what they're after on their sites.

Consumer Services

Web-based services

  • Google offers Gmail for email,Google Calendar for time-management and scheduling.
  • Google Docs, Sheets and Slides for productivity.
  • Google Photos for photo storage and sharing.
  • Google for Jobs is an enhanced search feature that aggregates listings from job boards and career sites.
  • Some Google services are not web-based. Google Earth, launched in 2005, allowed users to see high-definition satellite pictures from all over the world for free through a client software downloaded to their computers.

Software

  • Google develops the Android mobile operating system.
  • smartwatch, television, car , and Internet of things-enabled smart devices variations.

Hardware

  • In January 2010, Google released Nexus One, the first Android phone under its own brand.
  • In July 2013, Google introduced the Chromecast dongle, which allows users to stream content from their smartphones to televisions.
  • In June 2014, Google announced Google Cardboard, a simple cardboard viewer that lets user place their smartphone in a special front compartment to view virtual reality (VR) media.
  • Nest Wifi (originally Google Wifi), a connected set of Wi-Fi routers to simplify and extend coverage of home Wi-Fi.

Technology Analysis

PageRank Technology

Google's technology uses the collective intelligence of the web to determine a page's importance. There is no human involvement or manipulation of results, which is why users have come to trust Google as a source of objective information untainted by paid placement.

Hypertext-Matching Analysis

Google's technology analyzes the full content of a page and factors in fonts, subdivisions and the precise location of each word. Google also analyzes the content of neighboring web pages to ensure the results returned are the most relevant to a user's query.
